ISO 27001 Implementation Roadmap

Getting Started

Implementing an Information Security Management SYstem (ISMS) that complies with ISO 27001 can be a complex and overwhelming process. It will involve participation from every member of the organization, from top-level management to the Finance, Human Resources, IT, and other teams.

If your organization is attempting to implement ISO 27001 simply curiousity's sake in attempt to identify risk expsoure, or your organization is intending to achieve an accredited certification to develop business relationships, the entire process can is made even more difficult due to the nuances of the ISO audit requirements and how they apply to your organization's specific circumstances. Failure to properly understand how one particular requirement shouyld be applied can have significantly deleterious impacts on certification, up to audit failures and business losses.

Thusly, how the organization approaches ISO 27001 implementation is entirely contingent on experienced expertise that understands the process and is innately familiar with the ISO Standard itself.

A typical ISO 27001 implementation project

First off and, most importantly, there are no 2 projects that are the same. This is is for many reasons, such as organ izational complexity, it's existing posture of information security, the level of information security awareness, etc.

FireClave offers a variety of services beginning from a preliminary organizational self-assessment to a comprehensivley applied process with the end-result of an ISO 27001 certification.

That said, generally, the implementation process would include

  • Gap Analysis: Broadly identifies organizational degree of risk expsoure.
  • Determination of project scope and objectives
  • Detailed risk ssessment identification which assesses each risk and evaluates the degree of organizational exposure.
  • Building a risk response plan that incorporates the identifiedcontrol measures that either mitigates risk expsoure elements or accepts it.
  • Drafting and publishing a Risk Treatment Plan and the accompanying Statement of Applicability which highlights and discusses each risk exposure selection, mitigation, and provides an explanation for each risk expsoure element that is excluded from mitigation.
  • Authoring the series of mandatory ISO 27001 documents which includes individually relevant policies, procedures, and plans.
